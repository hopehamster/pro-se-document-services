Client Question Hub

What is a Registered Document Preparer in Nevada?

A Registered Document Preparer is a non-attorney professional authorized under NRS 240A to assist self-represented litigants with typing, formatting, and filing legal documents at their specific direction. We are registered with the State of Nevada and comply with all statutory requirements.

What is the difference between a Document Preparer and a Paralegal?

In Nevada, a Document Preparer is registered to assist the public directly. A Paralegal typically works under an attorney. Our team has the experience of paralegals but is registered to help you directly — without the cost of a full legal retainer.

Can you represent me in court?

No. We are not attorneys and cannot provide legal advice, legal opinions, or courtroom representation. We prepare the professional paperwork you need to represent yourself (Pro Se). If your case requires legal strategy or courtroom advocacy, you should consult a licensed attorney.

Can you help with Federal Court filings?

Yes. We specialize in Federal jurisdiction documents, including Civil Rights (Section 1983) complaints, Federal Habeas Corpus petitions, and appellate filings for the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals and the U.S. District Court of Nevada.

Do I need an attorney for my case?

Many procedural matters can be handled Pro Se if the documents are correctly drafted and filed. However, if you need legal advice, courtroom representation, or strategic guidance on the merits of your case, you must hire a licensed attorney. We can help you determine which documents you need.

How long does document preparation take?

Standard turnaround is 3-5 business days from the time we receive all necessary information. Rush services are available for urgent deadlines — if your filing deadline is within 48 hours, call us directly at 725-212-5305.

What areas of Nevada do you serve?

We serve all of Nevada, including Clark County (Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas), Washoe County (Reno, Sparks), and all rural counties. For Federal filings, we serve all 50 states through the U.S. District Court system.

How much does document preparation cost?

Pricing depends on the complexity and volume of documents needed. We provide a clear, flat-rate quote during your initial consultation — no hidden fees, no hourly billing surprises. Most single filings start at a competitive rate well below attorney fees for comparable work.
